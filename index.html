About Dr. Maya Reynolds

I'm a licensed clinical psychologist based in Santa Monica, California, offering therapy for adults who feel overwhelmed by anxiety, stress, or the lingering effects of past experiences. Many of the people I work with are high-achieving, thoughtful, and self-aware—but internally feel exhausted, stuck in overthinking, or emotionally on edge.

My work often focuses on anxiety, panic, trauma, and burnout. Clients frequently come to me feeling "functional" on the outside while quietly struggling with constant worry, tension in their body, difficulty sleeping, or a sense that they're always bracing for something to go wrong.

Therapeutic Approaches

I combine evidence-based methods with depth-oriented work to help you find relief, develop insight, and build resilience.

EMDR Therapy

Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR) is a powerful approach for processing traumatic memories and reducing their emotional charge. Whether you've experienced a single traumatic event or complex, long-standing patterns, EMDR helps your brain reprocess difficult experiences so they no longer control your present life. This method is paced carefully with an emphasis on safety and stabilization.

Anxiety & Panic Treatment

Using cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) and mindfulness-based approaches, I help clients understand and manage anxiety, panic attacks, and constant worry. We'll work together to identify triggers, challenge anxious thought patterns, and develop practical coping strategies. You'll learn to recognize the physical signs of anxiety and build tools to feel more regulated in your daily life—not just during therapy sessions.

Burnout & High-Pressure Support

Many of my clients are entrepreneurs, creatives, and professionals who've spent years pushing through stress and perfectionism. Therapy becomes a space to slow down, reconnect with yourself, and develop more sustainable ways of living and working. We address high internal pressure, disconnection from self, and the exhaustion that comes from constantly achieving without room to rest or reflect.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if therapy is right for me? +
If you're feeling overwhelmed, stuck in patterns of anxiety or overthinking, or if past experiences continue to affect your present life, therapy can help. Many of my clients are high-functioning but internally exhausted—therapy provides a space to slow down, process what you're experiencing, and develop sustainable ways to move forward. I offer a free initial consultation to discuss your concerns and determine if we're a good fit.
What can I expect in our first session? +
The first session is about understanding what brings you to therapy and getting to know each other. I'll ask about your current concerns, background, and what you're hoping to achieve. This is also your time to ask questions and see if my approach feels right for you. There's no pressure—therapy works best when you feel respected, understood, and actively involved in the process.
Do you accept insurance? +
I am an out-of-network provider and can provide superbills for potential insurance reimbursement. Many clients find that their PPO plans cover a significant portion of out-of-network services. I'm happy to discuss your insurance benefits and help you understand your coverage options. My standard rate is $200 for 50-minute individual sessions.
How long does therapy typically last? +
The length of therapy varies based on your individual needs and goals. Some clients benefit from shorter-term, focused work over several months, while others find value in longer-term support. We'll regularly check in about your progress and adjust our approach as needed. My goal is not just symptom relief, but helping you develop insight, resilience, and a stronger relationship with yourself over time.
What makes your approach different? +
I combine practical, evidence-based tools with depth-oriented work. Sessions are structured enough to feel supportive while leaving space for reflection and genuine insight. I understand the realities of living in a fast-paced environment—many of my clients are high-achievers who need therapy that respects both their intelligence and their exhaustion. I address both the emotional and physiological sides of anxiety and trauma, not just surface-level symptoms.
